What is a Prime Number?

A prime number is a natural number greater than 1 that has no positive divisors other than 1 and itself.

In other words, if a number n > 1 cannot be formed by multiplying two smaller natural numbers, it is prime.

Examples: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13 are primes. 4, 6, 8, 9, 10 are not.
